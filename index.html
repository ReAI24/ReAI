1st Workshop on Responsible Artificial Intelligence

ReAI

Workshop at SETN 2024, the 13th Conference on Artificial Intelligence, organized by the Hellenic Artificial Intelligence Society (EETN)

September 11-13, University of Piraeus, Athens, Greece

Description

Research in AI promises to bring huge advances in all aspects of human life, from groundbreaking discoveries in medicine and self-driving cars to the arts. However, it also raises serious ethical concerns. The RAI workshop will focus on the foundations, principles, and practices of responsibly developing and deploying AI systems, addressing issues of fair and unbiased use, transparency, explainability, respect for human autonomy, privacy, accountability, safety and trustworthiness.

Besides covering technical aspects, the workshop will also provide a broader interdisciplinary perspective by including legal, social, and ethical perspectives, aiming to provide participants with a well-rounded understanding of the implications and considerations surrounding AI beyond just its technical implementation.

Topics (not limited to):

  • Bias and fairness in AI systems
  • Transparency, interpretability and explainability (XAI)
  • AI regulations
  • Privacy and accountability
  • Use cases

Submission Guidelines:

  • In order to prepare their manuscripts, the authors should follow the ACM SIG Proceedings guidelines, available at http://www.acm.org/publications/article-templates/proceedings-template.html/
  • SETN 2024 accepts submissions of two types:
    • full papers (6-10 pages)
    • short papers (2-4 pages)
  • All paper submissions will be handled electronically via the Easychair conference management system at the following address: TBA.
  • Papers submitted should report original work.
  • The review process for the Research Track papers will be double-blind, so authors should not reveal their identities.

Attendance and proceedings:

  • Full papers will be presented in an oral presentation session, whereas short papers will be presented in a pitching session, during which authors can give flash presentations/posters.
  • At least one of the authors of each workshop paper must complete full conference registration and deliver a presentation during the workshop.
  • The accepted Research Track papers will be included in the Conference Proceedings, which will be published by ACM (International Conference Proceedings Series) and available through the ACM Digital Library. Extended versions of selected high-quality Research Track papers will be considered for possible publication in special issues of prominent international AI journals.
